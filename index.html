October 3, 2024

2024 Nobel Prize in Physics Awarded for Neutrino Research

The Nobel Prize in Physics 2024 was awarded jointly to Pierre Auger and colleagues for their groundbreaking research into neutrinos — elusive particles that could unlock mysteries of the universe's structure. The awarded experiment, conducted at SNOLAB, used deep-underground detectors to gather critical data on particle behavior, furthering our understanding of astrophysics and particle physics.

September 18, 2024

AI-Generated Protein Structures Advance Drug Discovery

Scientists using DeepMind’s AlphaFold have successfully predicted structures of thousands of previously uncharacterized proteins. This advancement is expected to speed up drug discovery and deepen our understanding of various diseases by allowing researchers to model molecular interactions more efficiently.

August 10, 2024

James Webb Telescope Captures Image of Earliest Galaxy

The James Webb Space Telescope has captured an image of a galaxy formed just 300 million years after the Big Bang. This discovery offers new insights into galaxy formation and the conditions of the early universe. The galaxy, dubbed JADES-GS-z13-0, is the most distant ever observed.
